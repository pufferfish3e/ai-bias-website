Singapore is obsessed with AI.

No seriously. 57% of companies here say AI is critical to their work.
That's more than in the US, Europe, or the rest of Asia.

Guess What? It's not just big businesses—students, workers, and even content creators are using AI for everything, from writing code to crafting Instagram captions.

From productivity boosts to policy breaches, AI is becoming deeply embedded in the workplace.

However, there's a catch...

In late 2024, Singapore ran its first-ever AI safety challenge. Think of it like Capture the Flag...

but instead of breaking into servers, people tried to make AI reveal its biases.

Spoiler: It worked.

  • Tested 4 major AI models: Claude, Llama, SEA-LION, Aya
  • Across 9 countries
  • 300+ testers
  • Prompted AIs with real-life scenarios in multiple languages

And here's what they found:

Real Bias. Real Problem.

But thankfully, Singapore is taking action.

The government is working with AI companies to make sure their models are safe and fair.

They're also looking at how AI can help with things like healthcare, education, and public services.

So while AI is changing the game, Singapore is making sure it plays fair.
